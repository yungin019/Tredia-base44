# Global Market State — 5 Concrete Examples

## Rule Enforcement
These examples show VALID outputs that avoid all forbidden phrases:
- ❌ quiet, no major moves, nothing happening, neutral, mixed, uncertain, positive, negative, sentiment, performance

## Example 1: Strong Bullish Day

**MARKET STATE:**
Stocks rallying on broad sector strength, tech leading with yields falling as safe-haven demand eases

**BIAS:**
Risk-on accelerating — momentum building into close

**WATCH:**
- Breadth holding above 60% up
- Tech sector (QQQ) pushing new intraday highs
- Support at yesterday's close

---

## Example 2: Risk-Off Selloff

**MARKET STATE:**
Markets selling off sharply as geopolitical headlines trigger risk reduction across equities and commodities

**BIAS:**
Bearish momentum — downside follow-through likely

**WATCH:**
- VIX spiking above key levels
- Sector leadership breakdown (defensive outperforming)
- Support at 200-day moving average

---

## Example 3: Consolidation Day (Low Activity)

**MARKET STATE:**
Market consolidating after yesterday's gains, traders waiting for earnings season catalysts to drive next move

**BIAS:**
Holding pattern — watching for breakout direction

**WATCH:**
- Key resistance above current levels
- Volume confirmation needed for sustained move
- Early earnings prints for guidance

---

## Example 4: Pre-Fed Waiting Day (Low Activity)

**MARKET STATE:**
Stocks grinding sideways as traders pause ahead of Fed rate decision, positioning for volatility spike

**BIAS:**
Cautious — range-bound until policy signal

**WATCH:**
- Fed announcement timing
- Implied volatility curve for risk positioning
- Sector positioning ahead of decision

---

## Example 5: Post-Earnings Rotation Day

**MARKET STATE:**
Market rotating out of mega-cap winners into beaten-down value, earnings beats triggering selective rallies in overlooked sectors

**BIAS:**
Broadening participation — small-cap and value leading

**WATCH:**
- Earnings beat ratios holding strong
- Sector rotation breadth (leadership change)
- Earnings guidance for forward momentum

---

## Validation Checklist

✅ **All examples avoid:**
- "market quiet" or "no major moves"
- "neutral" or "mixed" sentiments
- Generic filler words
- Empty-feeling language

✅ **All examples describe:**
- Visible, concrete market action
- Specific directional bias
- Actionable watch points

✅ **All examples use:**
- Active verbs (rallying, selling, consolidating, rotating, waiting, pausing, grinding)
- Human trader language
- 2-second comprehension rule
